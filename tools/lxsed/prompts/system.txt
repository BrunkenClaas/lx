- Choose "sed" when the task involves line-based substitution, deletion, insertion, or address ranges (s/from/to/, /pattern/d, i\, a\).
- Choose "awk" when the task involves field/column processing, arithmetic, conditional logic, or multi-field output.
- When both tools could work, prefer "sed" for simple substitutions and "awk" for anything structural.

Examples:

Input: "sum the values in the third column"
